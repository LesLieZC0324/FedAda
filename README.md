# FedAda

Client-edge-cloud Federated Learning (CEC-FL), which alleviates the communication bottleneck of traditional cloud-centric Federated Learning (FL) to some extent through edge computing, is becoming an increasingly popular paradigm for training machine learning models on large-scale distributed data. Considering limited resources, existing CEC-FL methods optimize the communication frequency of participating clients and edges to control local updating and model aggregation to achieve a trade-off between training quality and efficiency. However, in heterogeneous environments, these methods still suffer from unsatisfactory training performance due to the synchronization barrier. In this paper, we propose FedAda, a training-efficient CEC-FL method that achieves better training quality and efficiency by adaptively adjusting the communication frequency for both clients and edges. We theoretically analyze the relationship between model convergence and communication frequency, and on this basis, we propose an algorithm to calculate the communication frequency consistent with the node capability to improve the convergence accuracy and convergence speed. Simulation results validate the effectiveness and efficiency of FedAda and show that FedAda improves the model accuracy by up to 16% and reduces the training completion time by up to 4.7× compared to previous works.
